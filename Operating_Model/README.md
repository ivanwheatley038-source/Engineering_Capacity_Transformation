# Operating Model

## Objective

As the engineering organization expanded, organizational structure alone was not sufficient to sustain increasing delivery capacity.

A common operating model was established to ensure every team executed work through consistent processes, governance, and engineering practices regardless of product ownership or delivery methodology.

The objective was to create a repeatable delivery system capable of scaling across multiple engineering teams while maintaining transparency, predictability, and quality.

---

# Design Principles

The operating model was designed around several guiding principles.

- One standardized intake process
- Clearly defined ownership throughout the delivery lifecycle
- Consistent engineering governance
- Flexible execution using Scrum or Kanban where appropriate
- Shared engineering standards across all teams
- Continuous visibility into work throughout the delivery lifecycle

---

This common lifecycle enabled work to move predictably through the organization while allowing individual teams to execute using delivery methods best suited to their products.

---

# Delivery Framework

As engineering capacity increased, multiple delivery teams operated simultaneously under a shared engineering framework.

The operating model standardized:

- Work Intake
- Solution Refinement
- Architectural Review
- Delivery Planning
- Team Assignment
- Sprint Planning
- Kanban Flow
- Scrum Delivery
- Release Coordination
- Continuous Improvement

Rather than creating independent processes for each team, a common framework ensured consistency while allowing teams to maintain ownership of their respective products.

---

# Agile Delivery

The operating model intentionally supported multiple Agile execution models.

Scrum was used where iterative product development benefited from planned sprint execution.

Kanban was used where continuous delivery and operational responsiveness provided greater business value.

This hybrid approach allowed delivery practices to align with the nature of the work rather than forcing every team into the same methodology.

---

# Enterprise Alignment

The operating model was designed to integrate with broader enterprise planning while preserving team autonomy.

The framework supported:

- Quarterly planning
- Cross-team dependency management
- Portfolio prioritization
- Capacity planning
- Enterprise release coordination

This enabled engineering teams to remain aligned with organizational objectives while maintaining flexibility in day-to-day execution.

# Leadership Principles

Several principles guided development of the operating model.

- Standardize the system—not the creativity of engineers.
- Make ownership explicit.
- Reduce friction between teams.
- Design processes that scale with organizational growth.
- Improve visibility without increasing bureaucracy.
- Allow delivery methodology to fit the work rather than forcing a single approach.

---

# Outcome

The operating model provided a common delivery framework across the engineering organization.

Combined with organizational redesign and standardized engineering practices, it enabled engineering teams to collaborate effectively, scale predictably, and continuously increase delivery capacity over multiple years.

The operating model became the foundation upon which engineering standards, portfolio planning, and measurable delivery improvements were built.
