# Engineering Capacity Transformation
### Scaling Software Delivery by ~350% While Growing Engineering Capacity by ~150%

## Executive Summary

Between 2019 and 2024, I led the transformation of a software engineering organization responsible for delivering enterprise sales capabilities.

During this period, the organization increased annual delivery capacity from **1,292** accepted story points in 2019 to approximately **6,000** accepted story points in 2024—an increase of approximately **364%**—while growing engineering headcount by approximately **150%**. During this period, the quantity of committed and delivered projects increased proportionally at same rate.

While the metrics are significant, this repository is intended to document **how** those results were achieved.

The transformation was not driven by asking teams to work harder. Instead, it was accomplished through intentional organizational design, standardized engineering practices, scalable delivery processes, and continuous improvement over multiple years.

---

# Transformation Overview

The transformation consisted of five major initiatives working together.

## 1. Organizational Design

The engineering organization evolved from a single delivery team into multiple product-focused teams with clear ownership boundaries and accountability.

The team structure was intentionally designed to:

- Improve ownership
- Reduce delivery bottlenecks
- Increase parallel work streams
- Balance specialization with collaboration
- Scale leadership without sacrificing team autonomy

Documentation:
- Organization evolution
- Team structure diagrams

---

## 2. Delivery Operating Model

As the organization expanded, consistent execution became increasingly important.

A common operating model was established that standardized:

- Work Intake
- Solution Refinement
- Architectural Review
- Team Assignment
- Scrum Delivery
- Kanban Delivery
- SAFe Program Integration

The objective was to create one repeatable delivery system capable of supporting multiple engineering teams while maintaining transparency and predictability.

Documentation:
- Workflow diagrams
- Delivery process maps

---

## 3. Engineering Standards

To improve consistency across multiple teams and leaders, common engineering standards were introduced.

These included reusable templates and governance artifacts such as:

- Definition of Ready
- Definition of Done
- Project Intake
- Design & Estimation
- Sprint Closure Reporting

These standards reduced variation, improved onboarding, and enabled leaders to operate from a common framework.

Documentation:
- Templates
- Governance artifacts

---

## 4. Portfolio Delivery

Increasing engineering capacity only creates value if additional business outcomes can be delivered.

As delivery capacity increased, portfolio planning matured alongside it, enabling substantially more committed initiatives to be executed each year.

Roadmaps contained within this repository demonstrate the increasing scope of committed business delivery from 2019 through 2024.

Documentation:
- Annual Roadmaps
- Portfolio Planning

---

## 5. Measured Outcomes

The combined effect of organizational, process, and leadership improvements resulted in sustained increases in delivery capacity over multiple years.

| Year | Accepted Story Points | Growth vs. 2019 |
|------|----------------------:|----------------:|
| 2019 | 1,292 | Baseline |
| 2020 | 1,131 | -13% |
| 2021 | 2,165 | +68% |
| 2022 | 3,663 | +184% |
| 2023 | 4,601 | +256% |
| 2024 | 6,000 | +364% |

The initial decrease in 2020 reflects the investment period during which organizational restructuring and foundational improvements were implemented. Those changes established the platform for sustained growth over the following four years.
